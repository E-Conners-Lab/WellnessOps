# Report Structure — WellnessOps

## Overview

The client report is the primary deliverable of the audit. It must be clean, branded, and sound likethe practitioner wrote it personally. The system generates a draft;the practitioner reviews, overrides scores, and edits language before finalizing.

---

## Report Sections

### Header
- Client name (or anonymized code)
- Date of audit
- Audit tier: Core Audit or Extended Audit
- Practitioner: a wellness practitioner
- WellnessOps branding

### Section 1 — Overall Score

Score out of 100 with qualitative label:

| Range | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 90–100 | Thriving | Your environment is actively working for you |
| 75–89 | Intentional | Strong foundation with clear gaps worth addressing |
| 60–74 | Developing | Some intention present but significant friction points |
| 45–59 | Misaligned | Your environment and goals are pulling in opposite directions |
| Below 45 | Survival Mode | Your space is working against you in ways you may have stopped noticing |

### Section 2 — Category Scores

Each category scored 1–10 with the following format:

```
CATEGORY NAME
Score: X/10
Status: [Qualitative label based on score]
What I observed: [the practitioner's specific observations from the audit]
Why it matters: [Supporting science or philosophy from knowledge base]
How to close the gap: [Specific prioritized recommendations]
Products that help: [Vetted product recommendations from Domain 3]
Who can help: [Partner or vendor referral from Domain 7 if relevant]
```

**Score label mapping:**
- 10: Exceptional
- 8–9: Strong
- 6–7: Adequate
- 4–5: Problematic
- 2–3: Significant issue
- 1: Critical

### Section 3 — Priority Action Plan

Top 5 actions ranked by impact. Pulled from the lowest scoring categories and highest leverage interventions. Format:

```
1. [Action] — [Which category it addresses] — [Expected impact]
2. ...
```

### Section 4 — What Changes When You Fix This

Written in the practitioner's voice. Not clinical. Paints a picture of what daily life looks and feels like when recommendations are implemented. This is the vision section that makes the report feel actionable, not just diagnostic.

### Section 5 — Recommended Partners and Products

Consolidated list of all product and partner referrals from the report in one place. Organized by category for easy reference.

### Section 6 — Next Steps

Clear instruction on what to do, in what order, and how to reachthe practitioner for the next tier of service if they want implementation support.

## PDF Export Requirements

- Clean, branded layout with WellnessOps identity
- Professional typography (not default PDF styling)
- Score visualizations (progress bars or radial charts for category scores)
- Photo thumbnails wherethe practitioner included photos in observations
- Table of contents for reports with extended audit categories
- Footer with page numbers and report date
- File name format: `{client_display_name}_Wellness_Audit_{date}.pdf`
